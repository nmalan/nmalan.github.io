About

I am a physical oceanographer and Senior Research Associate at the Climate Change Research Centre (CCRC), UNSW Sydney, where I work with Associate Professor Alex Sen Gupta. I'm an oceanographer interested in connecting things: environmental extremes to their impacts, the deep ocean to the continental shelf, the shelf to the coast, and physical dynamics to biological responses. My research spans marine heatwaves and their subsurface expression, cross-shelf exchange in boundary current systems, and coastal extremes. I take a tool-agnostic approach — drawing on moorings, gliders, ship-based observations, satellites, and regional and global ocean models, choosing the best instrument for the question at hand.

I completed my PhD in Physical Oceanography at the University of Cape Town in 2018, and joined UNSW as a postdoctoral researcher in the Coastal and Regional Oceanography Laboratory with Professor Moninya Roughan, where I worked on eddy-driven cross-shelf transport, coastal warming, and freshwater extremes along the East Australian Current. During this time I served as Deputy Leader of the IMOS NSW Moorings Facility, contributing to Australia's sustained coastal ocean observation network. I moved to the CCRC in July 2023 to focus on subsurface marine heatwaves. I maintain active research collaborations in South Africa, including with SAEON and the Nansen-Tutu Centre at UCT.

Alongside my research I convene the Oceania Marine Heatwave Seminar series, serve on the International Working Group on Marine Heatwaves and Australia's Marine National Facility Research Advisory Committee, and supervise postgraduate students at UNSW and the University of Cape Town.

Research

Subsurface Marine Heatwaves

Marine heatwaves are typically measured at the ocean surface, yet their subsurface expression can differ dramatically in intensity, timing, and spatial extent. My research investigates how heat anomalies penetrate below the mixed layer, their links to mesoscale eddies and boundary current dynamics, and their implications for deep-water ecosystems and species.

Western Boundary Currents

I have worked extensively on both the East Australian Current and the Agulhas Current system — studying eddy-driven cross-shelf transport, current meanders, long-term temperature trends, and the role of boundary currents in driving coastal climate extremes along the Australian and South African coastlines.

Cross-shelf Transport & Coastal Processes

Understanding how heat, nutrients, and biota are exchanged between the open ocean and the continental shelf is central to predicting coastal ecosystem responses to climate change. I combine multi-platform observations (moorings, Argo floats, satellite) with numerical models to quantify these exchanges and their drivers.

Ocean Observing Systems

Long-term, high-quality ocean observations underpin all of my research. As Deputy Leader of the IMOS NSW Moorings programme (2018–2023), I contributed to the design, management, and data stewardship of Australia's sustained coastal mooring network. I am committed to open science and reproducible, observation-based research.
